# Example Rationale Language for AI Itinerary Planner

## Purpose

These examples show the style of explanation the AI should use when justifying itinerary recommendations.

## General Style

The explanation should be:

- short
- clear
- specific
- grounded in the user's interests
- honest about trade-offs

## Good One-Line Activity Rationales

### Cultural rationale

“We included this because it strongly matches your interest in authentic local culture and offers a more hands-on experience than a standard sightseeing activity.”

### Community rationale

“This activity is a strong fit because it directly supports local partners while also giving you a more meaningful connection to the destination.”

### Environmental rationale

“This option aligns with your interest in nature and has a relatively lower environmental footprint than more resource-intensive alternatives.”

### Adventure rationale

“This activity was selected because it matches your preference for outdoor adventure and adds a higher-energy element to the itinerary.”

### Food rationale

“We chose this because it combines local food, cultural learning, and direct interaction with the people behind the experience.”

## Honest Trade-Off Language

### High environmental impact trade-off

“This activity is a good fit for your interest in adventure, though it has a higher environmental footprint than some of the other available options.”

### Weak community link trade-off

“This activity fits your preference for excitement, but it currently has limited direct community benefit compared with other experiences.”

### Balanced trade-off language

“We included this because it matches your stated priorities well, while surfacing the trade-off so you can decide whether it is right for you.”

## Whole-Itinerary Rationales

### Culture-focused itinerary

“This itinerary is designed around immersive cultural experiences, with an emphasis on activities that offer direct local connection and relatively low environmental impact.”

### Sustainability-focused itinerary

“This itinerary prioritizes lower-impact, educational experiences that help you explore the destination while better understanding its environmental and community context.”

### Mixed-interest itinerary

“This itinerary balances culture, nature, and light adventure so you experience different sides of the destination without overloading the schedule.”

### Adventure-focused itinerary

“This itinerary is built for a more active traveler, while still including transparency notes so you can weigh excitement against environmental and community considerations.”

## Comparison Language

When comparing alternatives, use phrasing like:

- “more directly supports local partners”
- “lighter environmental footprint”
- “stronger fit for your interest in culture”
- “higher-energy option”
- “more balanced choice for a shorter stay”

Do not use:

- “best”
- “perfect”
- “most sustainable” unless proven
- “eco-friendly” as a blanket label without context

## Suggested Final Explanation Format

Recommended structure:

1. one sentence on overall fit
2. one sentence on transparency-aware logic
3. one optional sentence on trade-offs if relevant

Example:
“This itinerary matches your interest in culture and food through hands-on local experiences. We prioritized activities that also offer stronger direct community benefit and relatively lower environmental impact. One higher-impact option was not prioritized because there were better-fitting alternatives for your stated preferences.”
